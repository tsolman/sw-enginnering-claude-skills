# OKR Framework for Product Teams

Based on Marty Cagan's "Inspired" — using OKRs to empower teams with outcomes, not outputs.

## Core Philosophy

> "Instead of the typical roadmap where management prescribes features, the team itself should be empowered to decide how to solve business problems."

OKRs shift focus from **features to ship** to **outcomes to achieve**.

---

## OKR Structure

### Objective
- **What:** Qualitative goal that inspires the team
- **Criteria:** Meaningful, memorable, aspirational
- **Timeframe:** Quarterly for teams, annual for organization

### Key Results
- **What:** Quantitative measures of progress toward the objective
- **Criteria:** Specific, measurable, time-bound
- **Count:** 1-3 per objective (more dilutes focus)

---

## Good vs. Bad OKRs

### Bad OKR (Output-focused)
```
Objective: Launch new checkout flow
Key Results:
- Ship checkout redesign by March 15
- Complete QA by March 10
- Get stakeholder approval by March 1
```

**Why it's bad:** Measures activities, not impact. Team could ship on time and still fail.

### Good OKR (Outcome-focused)
```
Objective: Make checkout effortless for customers
Key Results:
- Reduce checkout abandonment from 68% to 55%
- Decrease average checkout time from 4.2 min to 2.5 min
- Increase checkout completion on mobile from 45% to 65%
```

**Why it's good:** Team is empowered to find the best solution. Success means customer impact.

## OKR Cadence

| Level | Cadence | Scope |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Company | Annual | Strategic priorities for the year |
| Product | Annual | Product vision milestones |
| Team | Quarterly | Specific outcomes team will drive |

### Quarterly Rhythm

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Last week of quarter | Draft next quarter's OKRs |
| Week 1 | Finalize and align OKRs |
| Weeks 2-12 | Execute, measure, adjust tactics |
| Week 12 | Score OKRs, capture learnings |

## Writing Effective Objectives

### Objective Checklist
- [ ] Does it describe a meaningful outcome?
- [ ] Is it inspiring and memorable?
- [ ] Does it align with company/product strategy?
- [ ] Can the team influence this outcome?
- [ ] Is it achievable in the quarter (stretch but possible)?

### Objective Patterns

**Customer-focused:**
- "Make [user segment] successful at [job to be done]"
- "Remove friction from [customer journey]"
- "Delight customers with [experience]"

**Business-focused:**
- "Establish [product] as the leader in [category]"
- "Build foundation for [strategic capability]"
- "Prove [market/segment] viability"

**Team-focused:**
- "Become experts in [domain]"
- "Build confidence in [capability]"

---

## Writing Effective Key Results

### Key Result Checklist
- [ ] Is it measurable with a specific number?
- [ ] Does it have a clear baseline (current state)?
- [ ] Is the target ambitious but achievable (60-70% confidence)?
- [ ] Can we measure progress throughout the quarter?
- [ ] Does achieving this actually matter?

### Key Result Patterns

**Metric improvement:**
- "Increase [metric] from X to Y"
- "Reduce [metric] from X to Y"

**Threshold achievement:**
- "Achieve [metric] of X"
- "Maintain [metric] above X"

**Milestone-based (use sparingly):**
- "Validate [hypothesis] with [evidence threshold]"
- "Achieve product-market fit signal: [specific criteria]"

## OKR Anti-Patterns

### At the Objective Level

| Anti-Pattern | Problem | Better Approach |
|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| Feature as objective | "Launch X" | What outcome does X create? |
| Too vague | "Improve experience" | Improve what, for whom? |
| Too many | 5+ objectives | Focus on 1-3 max |
| Not inspiring | "Maintain stability" | What does success look like? |

### At the Key Result Level

| Anti-Pattern | Problem | Better Approach |
|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| Activity metric | "Ship 10 features" | What impact do features have? |
| Binary | "Launch checkout" | Measurable outcome of launch |
| Too easy | 100% confidence | Should be 60-70% confident |
| Unmeasurable | "Users love it" | NPS, retention, specific behavior |
| Lagging only | "Revenue" | Add leading indicators |

## Scoring OKRs

At end of quarter, score each Key Result:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0.0 - 0.3 | Didn't make meaningful progress |
| 0.4 - 0.6 | Made progress but fell short |
| 0.7 - 0.8 | Hit the target (ideal range) |
| 0.9 - 1.0 | Exceeded expectations (was target too easy?) |

**Objective Score:** Average of Key Result scores

### Healthy OKR Outcomes
- 70% of OKRs land in 0.6-0.8 range
- Some miss badly (good — you're stretching)
- Some exceed (great — understand why)
- None are 1.0 (targets were too easy)

## OKRs and Product Discovery

OKRs inform what to discover. Discovery informs how to achieve OKRs.

```
Company Strategy
      ↓
Product Vision
      ↓
Team OKRs (outcomes to achieve)
      ↓
Product Discovery (find solutions)
      ↓
Validated Backlog (solutions that work)
      ↓
Product Delivery (build solutions)
      ↓
Measure Key Results
```

### Using OKRs in Discovery

**Before discovery:**
- What Key Result are we trying to move?
- What's our hypothesis for moving it?

**During discovery:**
- Are we learning things that suggest different Key Results?
- Should we pivot our approach?

**After discovery:**
- Update confidence in achieving Key Results
- Adjust tactics or scope as needed

## OKRs vs. Roadmaps

| Aspect | Traditional Roadmap | OKR-Driven |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| Focus | Features to ship | Outcomes to achieve |
| Commitment | "We'll build X" | "We'll move metric Y" |
| Team role | Execute the plan | Solve the problem |
| Success | Shipped on time | Achieved outcome |
| Flexibility | Low (committed to features) | High (committed to outcomes) |

**Key insight:** OKRs make the roadmap a hypothesis, not a promise.

## Common Questions

**Q: What if we achieve the Key Result but it doesn't move the business?**
A: The Key Result was wrong. Learn and pick better metrics.

**Q: What if we discover the objective is wrong mid-quarter?**
A: Adjust. OKRs are tools, not contracts. Document the learning.

**Q: How do OKRs relate to sprint planning?**
A: Sprints are tactics. OKRs are strategy. Sprint work should contribute to Key Results.

**Q: Should individual contributors have OKRs?**
A: Cagan recommends team-level only. Individual OKRs often become task lists.
